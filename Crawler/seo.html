</li></ol><article class="learn-article" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Article"><h1>What is SEO?</h1><div class="mb-6"><p>Search engine optimization (SEO) is the practice of increasing the quantity and quality of traffic to your website through organic search engine results.</p><h2>What goes into SEO?</h2><p> To understand what SEO really means, let's break that sentence down and look at the parts: </p><ul><li>Quality of traffic. You can attract all the visitors in the world, but if they're coming to your site because Google tells them you're a resource for Apple computers when really you're a farmer selling apples, that is not quality traffic. Instead you want to attract visitors who are genuinely interested in products that you offer.</li><li>Quantity of traffic. Once you have the right people clicking through from those search engine results pages (SERPs), more traffic is better.</li><li>Organic results. Ads make up a significant portion of many SERPs. Organic traffic is any traffic that you don't have to pay for. </li></ul><p><img src="https://moz-static.s3.amazonaws.com/learn/seo/paid-vs-organic-listings.jpg?mtime=20170104131544"></p><p>Organic search traffic is specifically any unpaid traffic that comes from SERPs.</p><h2>How SEO works</h2><p>You might think of a search engine as a website you visit to type (or speak) a question into a box and Google, Yahoo!, Bing, or whatever search engine you're using magically replies with a long list of links to webpages that could potentially answer your question. </p><p>That's true. But have you ever stopped to consider what's behind those magical lists of links? </p><p>Here's how it works: Google (or any search engine you're using) has a crawler that goes out and gathers information about all the content they can find on the Internet. The crawlers bring all those 1s and 0s back to the search engine to build an index. That index is then fed through an algorithm that tries to match all that data with your query. </p><p>There are a lot of factors that go into a search engine's algorithm, and here's <a href="/blog/weighting-the-clusters-of-ranking-factors-in-google-analytics-whiteboard-friday">how a group of experts ranked their importance</a>:</p><p><img src="https://moz-static.s3.amazonaws.com/learn/seo/rank-factors-pie-2013-lrg.gif?mtime=20170104131408" alt="rank-factors-pie-2013-lrg.gif?mtime=20170104131408#asset:2285:url"><br></p><p>That's all the SE (search engine) of SEO. </p><p>The O part of SEO&mdash;optimization&mdash;is where the people who write all that content and put it on their sites are gussying that content and those sites up so search engines will be able to understand what they're seeing, and the users who arrive via search will like what they see. </p><p>Optimization can take many forms. It's everything from making sure the <a href="/learn/seo/title-tag">title tags</a> and <a href="/learn/seo/meta-description">meta descriptions</a> are both informative and the right length to pointing <a href="/learn/seo/internal-link">internal links</a> at pages you're proud of. </p><h2>Learning SEO</h2><p>This section of our site is here to help you learn anything you want about SEO. If you're completely new to the topic, start at the very beginning and read the <a href="/beginners-guide-to-seo">Beginner's Guide to SEO</a>. If you need advice on a specific topic, dig in wherever suits you. </p><p>Here's a general overview:</p><h4>Building an SEO-friendly site</h4><p>Once you're ready to start walking that SEO walk, it's time to apply those SEO techniques to a site, whether it's brand new or an old one you're improving. </p><p>These pages will help you get started with everything from selecting an SEO-friendly <a href="/learn/seo/domain">domain name</a> to best practices for <a href="/learn/seo/internal-link">internal links</a>. </p><h4>Content and related markup</h4><p>A site isn't really a site until you have content. But SEO for content has enough specific variables that we've given it its own section. <a href="/learn/seo/on-page-factors">Start here</a> if you're curious about keyword research, how to write SEO-friendly copy, and the kind of markup that helps search engines understand just what your content is really about. </p><h4>On-site topics</h4><p>You've already learned a lot about on-site topics by delving into content and related markup. Now it's time to get technical with information about <a href="/learn/seo/robotstxt">robots.txt</a>. </p><h4>Link-related topics</h4><p>Dig deep into everything you ever needed to know about links from <a href="/learn/seo/anchor-text">anchor text</a> to redirection. Read this series of pages to understand how and when to use nofollow and whether guest blogging is actually dead. If you're more into the link building side of things (working to improve the rankings on your site by earning links), go straight to the <a href="/beginners-guide-to-link-building">Beginner's Guide to Link Building</a>. </p><h4>Other optimization</h4><p>Congratulations! You've mastered the ins and outs of daily SEO and are now ready for some advanced topics. Make sure all that traffic has the easiest time possible converting with <a href="/learn/seo/conversion-rate-optimization">conversion rate optimization (CRO)</a>, then go micro level with <a href="/learn/local">local SEO</a> or take that site global with <a href="learn/seo/international-seo">international SEO</a>. </p><h3>The evolution of SEO</h3><p>Search engine algorithms change frequently and SEO tactics evolve in response to those changes. So if someone is offering you SEO advice that doesn't feel quite right, check in with the specific topic page. </p><p>For a more technical look at SEO, check out this <a href="/academy/what-is-seo">short video from Rand Fishkin</a>. </p><p>Go forth and SEO&hellip;</p><hr><h3>Keep learning</h3><ul><li><a href="https://moz.com/learn/seo/page-authority"></a><a href="https://moz.com/learn/seo/on-site-seo">What is On-Site SEO?</a></li><li><a href="https://moz.com/learn/seo/external-link"></a><a href="https://moz.com/learn/seo/off-site-seo">What is Off-Site SEO?</a></li><li><a href="https://moz.com/blog/how-to-rank">How to Rank: 25-Step Master Blueprint</a></li><li><a href="https://moz.com/beginners-guide-to-link-building">The Beginner's Guide to&nbsp;Link Building</a></li></ul></div><div class="mb-6"><h3>
